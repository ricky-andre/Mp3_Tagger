Rename Configuration Examples

These are some useful examples that should help to understand how the rename window can be used in some real examples. As usual, use the "try" button before executing the rename operation, if you are not sure of what you are going to do!

Example 1

Suppose that you have some files, a compilation of songs:

"Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

"Uriah Heep - Easy Living.mp3"

"Velvet Underground - I'm Waiting.mp3"

Suppose that you want to add numbers, and you want to change the order of the listed songs. Act on the arrows to move the files in the list until they have the order you want. Then, write the following configuration strings:

Insert: " - " at position: "0"

Insert numbers starting from: "1" at position: "0"

You can even select the leading '0' option, if you want. These will be the results:

With leading '0' option selected:

"01 - Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"02 - The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"03 - Uriah Heep - Easy Living.mp3"

"04 - Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"05 - Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"06 - Velvet Underground - I'm Waiting.mp3"

"07 - Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"08 - The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

Without leading '0' option selected:

"1 - Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"2 - The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"3 - Uriah Heep - Easy Living.mp3"

"4 - Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"5 - Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"6 - Velvet Underground - I'm Waiting.mp3"

"7 - Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"8 - The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

Example 2

Suppose that you have a compilation your friend gave to you. Suppose that some songs are missing or you already have them and you have deleted them. This is the situation:

"01 - Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"02 - The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"04 - Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"05 - Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"07 - Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"08 - The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

Some numbers are not consecutive and you don't like it. Put in this simple configuration:

Search string:      "< 1 > - < 2 > - < 3 >.mp3"

Replace string:     "< 2 > - < 3 >.mp3"

Insert: " - " at position: "0"

Insert numbers starting from: "1" at position "0"

The operations are made from top to down and from left to right. After the multireplace feature is applied, the files will have the following names:

"Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

After the string insertion, that is performed as the second operation, this is the situation:

" - Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

" - The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

" - Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

" - Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

" - Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

" - The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

After the number insertion, you will have this final result:

"01 - Fleetwood Mac - Albatross.mp3"

"02 - The Byrds - Eight Miles High.mp3"

"03 - Jefferson Airplane - White Rabbit.mp3"

"04 - Jimi Hendrix - Hey Baby.mp3"

"05 - Pink Floyd - Speak To Me, Breath.mp3"

"06 - The Move - I Can Hear The Grass Move.mp3"

That is exactly what you wanted!
